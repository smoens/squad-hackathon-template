# Energy and Atmosphere

Designing the emotional arc, physical environment, and facilitation moments that make a hackathon feel alive — not like a meeting with laptops.

## When to Use This Skill

- Planning hackathon atmosphere and event flow
- Designing energy levels across phases
- Preparing facilitation micro-moments
- Setting up physical or virtual spaces
- Planning celebrations, awards, and closing ceremonies
- Recovering energy during slumps

---

## The Energy Arc

Every hackathon has an emotional shape. Design it intentionally.

### Target Energy Levels by Phase

| Phase | Duration | Target Energy | Tone | Music Tempo |
|-------|----------|---------------|------|-------------|
| Arrival & Setup | 30-60 min | 🌟 Warm | Welcoming, curious | Chill lo-fi, ambient |
| The Ignition (Kickoff) | 20-30 min | 🔥 High | Exciting, bold | Upbeat, cinematic |
| Ideation | 60-90 min | 🧠 Focused | Creative, open | Lo-fi beats, no lyrics |
| Selection & Commit | 15-30 min | ⚡ Spike | Decisive, energizing | Building tension |
| Build Sprint 1 | 90-120 min | 🧠 Focused | Deep work, flow state | Lo-fi or silence (team choice) |

| Build Sprint 2 | 90-120 min | 🧠 Focused | Heads down, grinding | Lo-fi or silence |
| Demo Prep | 60-90 min | ⚡ Spike | Nervous energy, exciting | Energetic, motivating |
| Main Stage Demos | 45-90 min | 🏆 Crescendo | Showtime, pride | Walk-on music per team |
| Awards & Close | 20-30 min | 🌟 Warm | Gratitude, reflection | Emotional, uplifting |

## The 7 Designed Moments

### 1. The Ignition (Kickoff)

**Purpose:** Transform a room of strangers into a team with shared purpose.

**Structure (20-30 min):**
1. Music playing as people arrive (energetic, modern)
2. Facilitator welcome — 3 min max. Set the tone: "This isn't a workshop. This is a hackathon. We build things."
3. Customer sponsor speaks — 5 min. Why this matters to THEM. Personal, not corporate.
4. The Rules — 2 min. Three rules only: (1) Time-boxes are law. (2) "Yes, and..." (3) Demo or it didn't happen.
5. The Challenge drop — show the problem space in one powerful slide or statement
6. Team introductions — 30 seconds per person. Name + superpower only.

**Energy target:** 🔥 High. People should feel like something is about to happen.

### 2. Team Identity Ceremony

**Purpose:** Create belonging and playful competition.

**Structure (10-15 min, during formation or early ideation):**
1. Each team picks a name (2 min — force speed)
2. Each team creates a 10-second war cry or motto (3 min)
3. Each team presents — applause mandatory after each
4. Names go on a visible scoreboard / progress wall

**Why it works:** Named teams with identities work harder and have more fun. It's not silly — it's psychology.

### 3. The Spark (Ideation Opening)

**Purpose:** Break the "blank page" paralysis and flood the room with possibilities.

**Structure (5 min):**
1. Facilitator shares 3-4 "What If" provocations (from engagement-kickstart hypotheses)
2. "Your job for the next 60 minutes is to generate better ideas than these."
3. Hand out "What If" stimulus cards (from ideation-techniques skill)
4. Timer visible on screen. Music starts. Go.

**Energy target:** 🧠 Focused but excited. Pens moving within 60 seconds.

### 4. The Commit (Build Kickoff)

**Purpose:** Transform selected ideas into action with ceremony and urgency.

**Structure (5-10 min):**
1. Selected use cases announced — with fanfare
2. Each team lead states their plan in 30 seconds: "We're building [X] and it'll demo [Y]"
3. Countdown: "Build starts in 3... 2... 1... GO!" (play a start sound)
4. Clock starts on screen — visible countdown to demo time

**Energy target:** ⚡ Spike. Adrenaline. The race is on.

### 5. First Demo (Mid-Build Celebration)

**Purpose:** Break isolation, celebrate progress, re-energize.

**Structure (15-20 min, midway through build):**
1. Each team does a 2-minute "show what works RIGHT NOW" — no polish required
2. Audience reaction: applause, cheers, "ooh" moments encouraged
3. Facilitator highlights one impressive thing from each team
4. Quick stand-up: "What's your biggest risk for the next sprint?"

**Energy target:** ⚡ Spike. "We're actually building something real."

### 6. Main Stage (Demo Show)

**Purpose:** The showcase moment. Make every team feel like a startup on launch day.

**Structure (per team: 8-12 min):**
1. MC introduces team by name, reads a 1-line teaser
2. Walk-on music plays (10 seconds — team's choice or assigned)
3. Team presents: 5-beat arc (see demo-storytelling skill)
4. Audience Q&A: 2-3 minutes
5. Score cards collected (if judging)
6. Transition music while next team sets up

**Energy target:** 🏆 Crescendo. This is the peak. Everything built to this.

**Production details:**
- Microphone for presenters (even small rooms — it signals "this is a show")
- Screen transitions between teams (even a simple title slide)
- Visible timer for fairness
- Designated "audience plant" to ask the first question if silence falls

### 7. The Close (Emotional Wrap)

**Purpose:** Convert energy into commitment and warm memory.

**Structure (20-30 min):**
1. Awards ceremony (see below)
2. Customer sponsor reflection — "What surprised me today" (3-5 min, unscripted)
3. Each team: one sentence on "what we learned" (round-robin, 30 sec each)
4. Facilitator close — thank everyone, state what happens next, end on an emotional note
5. Group photo
6. Music plays as people mingle

**Energy target:** 🌟 Warm. People should leave feeling proud and connected.

## Atmosphere Toolkit

### Music Recommendations

| Phase | Genre / Vibe | Example Artists/Playlists | Notes |
|-------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Arrival | Chill lo-fi, coffee shop | Lo-fi Girl, Nujabes, Bonobo | Low volume, background |
| Kickoff | Cinematic, epic | Hans Zimmer, Two Steps from Hell | Brief — for energy injection |
| Ideation | Instrumental focus | Lo-fi hip hop, post-rock, ambient electronic | No lyrics — they distract writing |
| Build sprints | Team choice or silence | Varies | Offer a shared playlist teams can add to |
| Check-ins | Upbeat pop/rock | Current hits, classic crowd-pleasers | 1 song, high energy |
| Demo walk-on | Team-selected or genre mix | Eye of the Tiger, Thunderstruck, Lose Yourself | 10-15 seconds only |
| Awards | Triumphant | Olympic themes, movie score climaxes | For announcements |
| Closing | Warm, emotional | Acoustic, singer-songwriter, feel-good | Gratitude tone |

**Music rules:**
- Always have a portable speaker (don't rely on laptop speakers)
- Volume: background during work, foreground during moments
- No explicit lyrics
- Have a shared playlist link so people can add songs

### Room / Space Setup

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Layout | Cluster tables (4-6 per table), NOT classroom rows |
| Screens | 1 per team + 1 main presentation screen |
| Whiteboards | At least 1 per team (or large sticky note pads) |
| Power | Extension cords to every table — plan this |
| Progress wall | Visible wall/board showing all teams, phases, countdown |
| Parking lot | Dedicated flip chart for parked ideas |
| Snack station | Separate from work tables, encourages movement |
| Quiet zone | One corner/room for heads-down focus |

### Snack & Food Strategy

| Timing | What | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| Arrival | Coffee, tea, water, light pastries | Comfort, welcome |
| Mid-morning | Fresh fruit, granola bars | Sustained energy, no sugar crash |
| Lunch | Substantial but not heavy (wraps > pasta) | Heavy food = afternoon slump |
| Afternoon | Dark chocolate, nuts, more coffee | Energy recovery |
| Demo time | Nothing — clear the food | Focus on presentations |
| Closing | Celebratory snack (cake, cookies, pizza) | Reward energy |

**Rule:** Never run out of coffee. Ever.

### Progress Wall Design

A visible physical or digital board that shows:

```
│  ┌──────────┐    ┌──────────┐    ┌──────────┐│
│  │ Use case │    │ Use case │    │ Use case ││

```

Update every check-in. Teams update their own status.

## Awards Ceremony

### The 5 Awards

| Award | Criteria | Who Votes |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 🏆 **Customer's Choice** | Most valuable to the customer's business | Customer sponsor(s) |
| 🔄 **Best Pivot** | Team that adapted best when things went sideways | Facilitator + squad |
| 🤯 **"Holy Sh*t" Moment** | Single most impressive technical or creative moment | Audience popular vote |
| 📖 **The Storyteller** | Best demo narrative — made us feel the problem and solution | Judges panel |
| 👥 **People's Choice** | Audience favorite overall | Audience popular vote |

**Award ceremony format:**
1. Start with People's Choice (lightest)
2. Best Pivot (celebrates resilience)
3. The Storyteller (celebrates narrative)
4. "Holy Sh*t" Moment (celebrates wow factor)
5. Customer's Choice last (the main event)

**Rules:**
- Every team gets called on stage / recognized for SOMETHING
- No "participation trophies" — but genuine callouts for each team's strengths
- Physical tokens if possible (printed certificates, small trophies, silly prizes)
- Photos of every winning moment

## Energy Recovery Techniques

### Post-Lunch Energy Boost (5-10 min)

| Technique | Description | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Stand & stretch | Facilitator leads 2 min of standing, stretching | Any group |
| Walk & talk | Pairs walk a loop while discussing their use case | Small groups |
| Energy music blast | Play 1 upbeat song, everyone stands | Fun groups |
| Lightning demos | Show 3 inspiring 1-min videos of cool tech | Any group |
| The question | "What's the ONE thing you must demo today?" — 30 sec each | Re-focusing |

### Afternoon Slump Prevention

1. **Schedule the mid-build check-in** for 30 min after lunch — gives energy a boost
2. **Move the snack station** to a different location — forces people to walk
3. **Open windows** if possible — fresh air matters
4. **Change the music** — switch genres, increase tempo slightly
5. **Facilitator walkabout** — visit each team, ask questions, show interest

## Remote / Hybrid Adaptations

### Challenges

Remote hackathons lose ambient energy, spontaneous interaction, and physical presence. You must compensate deliberately.

### Compensating Techniques

| In-Person Element | Remote Equivalent |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Room energy | Cameras on, reactions encouraged (emoji, chat), facilitator energy 2x |
| Background music | Shared Spotify session in a background channel (optional join) |
| Physical progress wall | Digital board (Miro, FigJam, or Teams whiteboard) visible in a pinned tab |
| Snack station | "Snack break" scheduled pauses — encourage people to grab something |
| Walk-on music | Play via screen share during intros — yes, it still works |
| Whiteboards | Digital whiteboard per team |
| Group photo | Everyone waves on camera — screenshot |
| Awards | Screen share award slides, applause = emoji flood in chat |
| Hallway conversations | Dedicated "hallway" breakout room that's always open |

### Hybrid-Specific Rules

1. **Remote-first audio** — everyone wears a headset, even in-person people. No speakerphone.
2. **Chat is the equalizer** — remote people can't tap shoulders. Chat must be actively monitored.
3. **Camera on for all moments** — cameras optional during deep work, mandatory during ceremonies.
4. **Rotate who speaks first** — alternate between in-person and remote to prevent remote fade-out.
5. **Dedicated remote advocate** — one in-person person whose job is to voice remote team needs.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Instead |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| No music at all | Room feels like an exam hall | Even low ambient music transforms mood |
| Skipping ceremonies to "save time" | Kills team identity and energy | Ceremonies ARE the energy. They're not optional overhead. |
| Flat energy all day | People check out by afternoon | Design deliberate peaks and valleys |
| Food coma lunch | Heavy lunch = dead afternoon | Light lunch, protein-heavy, avoid carb crash |
| No visible countdown | Urgency evaporates | Keep the clock visible at all times |
| Awards as afterthought | Feels arbitrary and rushed | Plan them, rehearse the sequence, make it a show |
| Ignoring remote participants | Remote people disengage silently | Active inclusion: name them, ask them, make space |
