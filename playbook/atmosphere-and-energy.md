# Atmosphere & Energy

A hackathon isn't a meeting. It's an experience. The difference between "that was productive" and "that was unforgettable" lives in the energy design — the intentional moments, the atmosphere, the emotional arc.

This is the guide for facilitators who want to run a *great* hackathon, not just a good one.

---

## The Energy Arc

A hackathon has a heartbeat. Map your energy design to the natural rhythm of the day.

```
Energy
  ▲
  │     🔥         ⚡                        🎤    🎆
  │    Ignition   The Spark                Demo   Close
  │   ╱        ╲       ╱╲                  ╱  ╲  ╱
  │  ╱   Team   ╲     ╱  ╲    First Demo  ╱    ╲╱
  │ ╱  Identity  ╲   ╱    ╲      ↗       ╱
  │╱              ╲ ╱      ╲   ╱        ╱
  │     🤝  The    ╳  Deep  ╲ ╱    🎯
  │         Commit  ╲ Focus  ╲  Build 2
  │                  ╲       ╱
  │                   ╲     ╱
  │                    ╲   ╱
  │                     ╲ ╱  ← lowest energy = deep build
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────►  Time
   Day 1 AM         Day 1 PM        Day 2 AM    Day 2 PM
```

| Phase | Energy Target | Facilitator Note |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Arrival** | Warm, curious | Music playing. Coffee ready. No dead silence when people walk in. |
| **Kickoff** | High, directional | You set the pace. Stand. Project. Own the room. |
| **Ideation** | Creative, buzzy | Allow chaos. Sticky notes flying = success. |
| **Build start** | Focused, determined | Energy dips here — that's normal. Protect the focus. |
| **Deep build** | Quiet, deep | Lowest energy point. Don't interrupt. Flow state is gold. |
| **Demo prep** | Nervous, energizing | Rehearsal anxiety is good energy. Channel it. |
| **Demo show** | Peak energy | This is the main event. Treat it like one. |
| **Close** | Warm, reflective | Slow down. Name what was achieved. Let it land. |

## 7 Designed Moments

These are the moments people remember. Design them deliberately.

### 1. 🔥 Ignition — The First 5 Minutes

**When:** Day 1, 9:00 AM
**What happens:** Music fades. Facilitator stands. "Welcome to [Customer]'s hackathon. In the next [1/2] days, you're going to build something that doesn't exist yet."

**Why it works:** First impressions anchor everything. If the first 5 minutes feel like a meeting, the whole event feels like a meeting.

**Facilitator script:**
> *"Look around the room. Every person here was chosen for a reason. By the end of [tomorrow/today], you'll have built something you can show your boss on Monday — something that solves a real problem, with real data. Let's make it count."*

Pause. Let it land. Then transition to agenda.

### 2. 🤝 Team Identity — The Name

**When:** Day 1, after ideation, when teams form around use cases
**What happens:** Each team picks a name. 60 seconds. No deliberation. First idea wins.

**Why it works:** A name creates belonging. "Team Predictive Maintenance" is forgettable. "Team Crystal Ball" is memorable and creates in-jokes.

**Facilitator script:**
> *"You've got 60 seconds. Pick a team name. It can be ridiculous. Go."*

Write team names on the progress wall immediately.

### 3. ⚡ The Spark — Ideation Opener

**When:** Day 1, start of ideation session
**What happens:** Customer sponsor tells a war story — the last time the problem cost real time or money. Facilitator reframes it as a "What if..." question.

**Why it works:** A real story grounds brainstorming in reality. Abstract problems generate abstract solutions.

See [Ideation Cheat Sheet](ideation-cheat-sheet.md) for the full flow.

### 4. ✊ The Commit — Announcing What You're Building

**When:** Day 1, end of ideation/feasibility scoring
**What happens:** Facilitator announces the selected use cases with ceremony. Customer confirms.

**Why it works:** Public commitment creates accountability and excitement. The shift from "brainstorming" to "building" needs to be felt, not just said.

**Facilitator script:**
> *"Here's what we're building. Team [name] is tackling [use case]. Team [name] is building [use case]. [Customer], does this feel right?"*

Wait for their yes. Then: *"Let's go build it."*

### 5. 👀 First Demo — The Early Win

**When:** Day 1, end of afternoon (informal)
**What happens:** Each team shows 30 seconds of *something working* — even if it's just data loading or a chart rendering.

**Why it works:** Seeing progress kills doubt. "We've got nothing" anxiety dies instantly when someone shows *anything* on screen.

**Facilitator script:**
> *"Before we break — 30 seconds per team. Show us something. Anything. A chart, a query result, a button that works. Go."*

Clap after each one. Seriously.

### 6. 🎤 Main Stage — The Live Demo

**When:** Day 2 afternoon, the demo showcase
**What happens:** Full 5-Beat Arc demos with MC introduction, walk-on energy, audience engagement.

**Why it works:** This is the payoff. Every hour of preparation leads here. Treat it like a stage show.

**MC introduction template:**
> *"Next up — Team [name]. They tackled [problem in one sentence]. Please welcome [presenter names]."*

See [Demo Crafting](demo-crafting.md) for script structure.

### 7. 🎆 The Close — The Emotional Landing

**When:** Day 2, final 15 minutes
**What happens:** Facilitator names one highlight per team. Customer shares what surprised them. Collective moment of reflection.

**Why it works:** People remember endings. A strong close turns "that was cool" into "that changed how I think."

**Facilitator script:**
> *"Before we go — let me name what I saw. Team [name], you [specific achievement]. Team [name], you [specific achievement]. [Customer sponsor], what surprised you this week?"*

Let the customer talk. Their words are more powerful than yours.

> *"You built [number] working prototypes in [1/2] days. That's not normal. That's what happens when smart people focus on real problems. Thank you all."*

## Atmosphere Toolkit

### 🎵 Music

Music transforms dead air into energy. Use it.

| Phase | Vibe | Playlist suggestion |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| **Arrival** | Warm, upbeat | Indie pop, acoustic covers — coffee shop energy |
| **Ideation** | Creative, buzzy | Lo-fi beats, instrumental hip-hop |
| **Build (focus)** | Deep concentration | Lo-fi study beats, ambient electronic — no lyrics |
| **Transitions** | Energy bump | Upbeat instrumental (movie soundtracks work great) |
| **Demo walk-on** | Main stage energy | Something with swagger — think sports arena entrance |
| **Close** | Reflective, warm | Acoustic, piano — cinematic calm |

> **Rule:** Music plays on entry and during transitions. Fade it out when someone speaks. Never compete with voices.

### 🏗️ Space Design

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Clusters of tables — teams face each other | Classroom rows — people stare at backs of heads |
| Demo stage visible from the start — builds anticipation | Hide the presentation area until demo time |
| Progress wall — team names, use case cards, status updates | Bare walls — invisible progress kills momentum |
| Standing meeting area — for quick check-ins | Every conversation sitting down |
| Whiteboards / flip charts within arm's reach | Single whiteboard at the front that only the facilitator uses |

### 🍕 Food & Energy

| Time | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| **Morning** | Coffee on arrival. No waiting for coffee = no grumpy starts. |
| **10:30 AM** | Fruit + protein snacks (nuts, cheese). Sugar crash = ideation crash. |
| **Lunch** | Substantial but not heavy. Avoid the post-lunch food coma. |
| **2:30 PM** | Afternoon energy hit — chocolate, energy bars, fresh coffee round. This is when build energy dips. |
| **4:00 PM** | Light snacks for demo prep. Nervous energy needs fuel. |

> **Coffee ritual:** Make the afternoon coffee round an event. Facilitator walks around with a tray: *"Refuel break — 5 minutes, then we push to the finish."* It's a mini energy injection.

## 🏆 Awards Ceremony

Run this in 5 minutes, right before The Close. Keep it fast, fun, and genuine.

### The 5 Categories

| Award | What it recognizes | Selection |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| **🏆 Most Impactful** | Highest potential business value | Customer vote |
| **🔬 Most Innovative** | Most creative or unexpected approach | Squad vote |
| **📊 Best Data Story** | Best use of data to tell a compelling narrative | Architect picks |
| **🎤 Best Demo** | Most compelling presentation | Audience applause meter |
| **💪 Grit Award** | Team that overcame the biggest obstacle | Facilitator picks |

### How to run it

1. Announce all 5 categories at once
2. Name the winner of each — one sentence why
3. No physical prizes needed (but stickers, badges, or silly trophies are fun)
4. Group photo after

> Keep it light. The real prize is the work they built. Awards are the cherry.

## 💻 Remote / Hybrid Adaptations

Energy is harder to maintain virtually. Here's how to compensate.

### Virtual Energy Techniques

- **Camera-on culture** — Make it the norm from minute one. Your camera is on first.
- **Name-calling** — In round-robin, call on people by name. Don't wait for volunteers.
- **Reaction emojis** — After each demo or share, ask for a 👏 in chat. Visual feedback replaces applause.
- **Music in waiting rooms** — Play arrival music in the meeting lobby. No dead silence.
- **Breakout rooms for build phase** — One room per team. Facilitator drops in for 5-min check-ins.

### Virtual Progress Wall

Use Miro, FigJam, or a shared whiteboard:
- Team names and use case cards on the board at all times
- Status markers: 🔴 Stuck → 🟡 In Progress → 🟢 Demo Ready
- Everyone can see everyone else's progress

### Hybrid Gotchas

- Remote participants speak first in round-robin (they get forgotten otherwise)
- One laptop = one camera on the room. Remote people need to see faces.
- Repeat all in-room comments to the remote audience
- Demo presenters share their own screen (don't project and point a camera)

## The Emotional Close

This is the last thing people hear. Make it count.

**Facilitator script — the full version:**

> *"We're almost at the end. Let me take 2 minutes to name what I saw this week.*
>
> *Team [name] — you started with [problem] and in [time], you built [what they built]. The moment [specific highlight] happened, I knew this wasn't just a prototype.*
>
> *Team [name] — you hit a wall on [challenge] and you didn't flinch. You pivoted to [solution] and the demo showed why that was the right call.*
>
> *[Repeat for each team — one specific observation each]*
>
> *[Customer sponsor name] — what surprised you?"*

**Let the customer talk.** Don't rush them. Their reflection is the most valuable feedback you'll get.

Then close:

> *"You built [number] working prototypes in [1/2] days. Real data. Real problems. Real solutions. That's not normal — that's what happens when the right people focus on work that matters.*
>
> *Thank you. Every one of you."*

Pause. Let it land. Then: logistics, next steps, goodbye.

> The best hackathons don't end with a slide. They end with a feeling.
