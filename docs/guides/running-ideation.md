# Running Ideation

Ideation is where the hackathon earns its ROI. A well-facilitated ideation session turns vague customer pain into concrete, demo-able use cases. A poorly run one burns your most valuable time on unfocused brainstorming.

**Who this is for:** Facilitators (Jared role) and anyone helping steer ideation.

**Time-box:** 2–3 hours for a 2-day hackathon, 1–1.5 hours for a 1-day.

---

## Pre-Session Preparation

Do this *before* the ideation session starts — ideally the day before.

### Materials Checklist

| Item | Owner | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| Printed/shared [Use Case Canvas](../../templates/use-case-canvas.md) (5–8 copies) | Facilitator | ☐ |
| Pre-filled hypothesis canvases (3–5 seeds) | Facilitator + Architect | ☐ |
| [Feasibility Scorecard](../../templates/feasibility-scorecard.md) (printed or projected) | Architect | ☐ |
| Sticky notes + markers (physical) or Miro/FigJam board (virtual) | Facilitator | ☐ |
| Timer visible to everyone | Facilitator | ☐ |
| Customer's [BRIEF.md](../../BRIEF.md) projected or printed | Facilitator | ☐ |

### Seed Use Cases

Prepare 3–5 use case hypotheses before the session. These aren't pre-built answers — they're **conversation starters** that help the customer's thinking take shape faster.

Write each one as a single sentence: *"What if [persona] could [outcome] by [approach]?"*

> **Tip:** Base your seeds on the customer pain points from the Brief. If you can't articulate their pain, you're not ready for ideation.

---

## Room / Virtual Setup

### Physical

- Whiteboard or wall space for canvases
- One table per breakout group (if >6 people)
- Timer on screen or wall
- Brief projected on second screen

### Virtual

- Miro or FigJam board pre-loaded with canvas templates
- Breakout rooms configured (if >6 people)
- Screen share permissions confirmed for all participants
- Chat channel for async notes

## Session Flow

### Phase 1: Context Setting (10–15 min)

1. **Recap the Brief** — Facilitator walks through business context and goals (2 min)
2. **Customer voice** — Ask the customer sponsor: *"What would make this hackathon successful for you?"* (5 min)
3. **Ground rules** — No idea is bad, time-boxes are sacred, feasibility comes later (1 min)
4. **Reveal seeds** — Show the 3–5 hypothesis canvases to prime the pump (2 min)

### Phase 2: Brainstorming (30–45 min)

**Structure: Silent → Share → Discuss**

| Step | Duration | What happens |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Silent write** | 8 min | Everyone writes use case ideas on sticky notes / canvas cards — one idea per note |
| **Round-robin share** | 10 min | Each person presents their ideas (30s each, no debate) |
| **Cluster** | 5 min | Group similar ideas together, name the clusters |
| **Discuss** | 15 min | Open discussion on top clusters — clarify, combine, refine |

> **Tip:** Enforce silent writing strictly. Without it, the loudest voice in the room dominates and you lose the best ideas from quieter participants.

**If you have more than 8 people:** Split into two groups for brainstorming, then merge for clustering.

### Phase 3: Voting and Prioritization (15–20 min)

**Dot voting:**
1. Each participant gets **3 dots** (stickers or virtual votes)
2. Vote for the use cases you think have the **highest customer value**
3. No lobbying during voting — silent again

**Rank by votes.** Take the top 4–6 candidates forward to feasibility scoring.

> **Tip:** Let the *customer* participants vote too. Their votes should weigh heavier informally — watch where they put their dots.

### Phase 4: Feasibility Scoring (30–45 min)

Use the [Feasibility Scorecard](../../templates/feasibility-scorecard.md) to evaluate each top candidate.

**How to run it:**

1. Project the scorecard framework so everyone can see the dimensions
2. Score **one use case at a time**, as a group
3. The **Architect** (Gilfoyle role) leads scoring — they ask the questions, the customer answers
4. For each dimension, ask: *"Given what we know right now, where does this land?"*
5. Record scores and notes

| Score Range | Assessment | What it means |
|:-----------:|-----------|---------------|
| 6 – 8 | 🟢 **Go** | High confidence — build a full PoC |
| 9 – 12 | 🟡 **Go with scope cut** | Feasible if you narrow the demo-able slice |
| 13 – 15 | 🟠 **Stretch** | Risky — only if high customer value |
| 16 – 18 | 🔴 **Redirect** | Pivot to a different approach |

### Phase 5: Decision and Assignment (10–15 min)

1. **Pick 1–3 use cases** to build (depends on format and squad size)
2. Assign each use case to a squad member pairing (Builder + Data Wrangler, typically)
3. The Facilitator confirms with the customer: *"We're going to build X, Y, and Z. Does that feel right?"*

> **Tip:** Always ask for customer confirmation. Even if the scoring is clear-cut, the customer feeling heard matters more than the math.

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | What happens | How to avoid it |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Skipping silent write** | One voice dominates, diversity of ideas drops | Enforce the silence — literally use a timer |
| **Scoring before brainstorming** | Ideas get killed before they're fully formed | Keep brainstorming and scoring as separate phases |
| **Too many use cases selected** | Build phase is scattered, nothing reaches demo quality | Max 3 use cases for a 2-day hack, 1-2 for a 1-day |
| **No customer voice in voting** | You build what the squad finds interesting, not what the customer needs | Explicitly ask customers to vote and weight their input |
| **Debating feasibility without data** | Arguments go in circles | If you can't score a dimension, mark it 🟡 and move on |
| **Running over time** | Build phase gets compressed, demos suffer | See [Time-Boxing](../principles/time-boxing.md) — cut the session, not the build |

## Time-Box Reference

| Ideation Phase | 1-Day Hackathon | 2-Day Hackathon |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Context setting | 10 min | 15 min |
| Brainstorming | 25 min | 40 min |
| Voting | 10 min | 15 min |
| Feasibility scoring | 25 min | 40 min |
| Decision + assignment | 10 min | 15 min |
| **Total** | **~1.5 hours** | **~2.5 hours** |
