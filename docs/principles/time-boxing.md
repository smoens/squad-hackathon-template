# Time-Boxing

Time-boxing is customer respect, not limitation. When a customer gives you one or two days of their team's time, every minute matters. A hackathon that runs over, meanders, or "just needs 30 more minutes" signals that you don't value their calendar.

---

## Philosophy

**A hackathon is not a project with a flexible deadline.** It's a performance with a fixed curtain time.

Time-boxing forces three things that make hackathons better:

1. **Decisions happen.** When you have 45 minutes to score feasibility, you can't debate endlessly. You score, you move.
2. **Scope stays honest.** When you have 4 hours to build, you build the demo-able slice, not the dream architecture. See [Scope Management](scope-management.md).
3. **Energy stays high.** Fatigue is the enemy of good demos. Tight schedules keep momentum.

> The goal is not to fill every minute. The goal is to protect the minutes that matter.

---

## Rules for Time-Boxing Each Stage

### Formation (Pre-Hackathon)

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Brief must be complete **48 hours** before Day 1 | Gives the squad time to prepare seeds and accelerators |
| Formation checklist reviewed **24 hours** before | Catches missing prerequisites while there's time to fix them |
| No new scope introduced after Brief is locked | Formation is for preparation, not discovery |

### Ideation

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Silent write is **exactly 8 minutes** — timer visible | Protects quiet thinkers from being steamrolled |
| Feasibility scoring is **max 10 minutes per use case** | Prevents analysis paralysis — score with what you know |
| Ideation ends when the clock says so, not when "we're done" | Protects build time |

### Build

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| First checkpoint at **25% of build time** | Catches wrong directions early — if data isn't loading, pivot now |
| Hard stop for build at the scheduled time — no extensions | Demo prep needs its full allocation |
| If a use case isn't demo-able at 75% mark, **cut it** | Better to demo 2 things well than 3 things badly |

### Demo Prep

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Demo script must be written **before** the last build hour | You can't write a story about something that doesn't exist yet |
| One full rehearsal — timed — is **mandatory** | First-time presenters always run 30% over |
| Backup screenshots captured **before** rehearsal | Insurance against demo failures |

### Demo

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **5 minutes per use case** — hard stop | Customers lose attention after 5 minutes; switch use cases to keep energy |
| Q&A is time-boxed too | Prevents one question from eating the entire session |
| Facilitator holds the clock, not the presenter | Presenters always think they have more time |

### Wind-Down (Post-Hackathon)

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Retro within **48 hours** | Memory fades fast — capture while it's fresh |
| [HANDOFF.md](../../HANDOFF.md) completed before the squad disbands | Don't leave the customer without a next-steps document |
| Knowledge extraction within **1 week** | Patterns and learnings belong in accelerator packs, not in someone's head |

## How to Enforce Time-Boxes Gracefully

Time-boxing only works if someone enforces it — and that someone is the Facilitator.

| Technique | When to use |
|-----------|------------|
| **Visible timer** | Always. Project it, tape it to the wall, whatever — it should be impossible to ignore |
| **5-minute warning** | Before every transition: *"Five minutes left in brainstorming — finish your current thought"* |
| **Hard cut with redirect** | When time expires: *"Great energy — let's carry that into scoring. We move in 60 seconds."* |
| **Parking lot** | For tangents: *"Important point — I'm adding that to the parking lot and we'll address it in the next slot"* |
| **Private nudge** | For someone dominating time: quiet word during a break, not a public callout |

> **Tip:** Frame time-boxing as *enabling*, not restricting. *"We're moving to build now so you have maximum time to create something amazing"* lands better than *"Time's up."*

---

## What to Do When You Run Over

It happens. Here's the recovery playbook:

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Ideation runs 15 min over | Borrow from the break, not from build. Shorten the next break by 15 min |
| Build is behind at the 75% mark | Cut the weakest use case. Redistribute the squad to strengthen remaining demos |
| Demo prep is rushed | Skip the fancy slides. A narrated live demo with a backup screenshot deck is fine |
| A demo runs over 5 minutes | Facilitator cuts in: *"Let's take questions on this one and move to the next demo"* |
| The whole day is behind | Facilitator resets: *"Here's our adjusted schedule for the afternoon"* — communicate openly |

**The cardinal rule:** Never borrow from demo time. The demo is the customer's payoff. Everything else exists to serve it.

## Example Schedules

### 1-Day Hackathon (8 hours)

| Time | Phase | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 – 09:15 | Welcome + Brief recap | 15 min |
| 09:15 – 10:45 | Ideation (brainstorm → score → decide) | 1.5 hrs |
| 10:45 – 11:00 | ☕ Break | 15 min |
| 11:00 – 12:30 | Build — Sprint 1 | 1.5 hrs |
| 12:30 – 13:15 | 🍕 Lunch | 45 min |
| 13:15 – 15:15 | Build — Sprint 2 | 2 hrs |
| 15:15 – 15:30 | ☕ Break | 15 min |
| 15:30 – 16:30 | Demo prep + rehearsal | 1 hr |
| 16:30 – 17:30 | Demos + Q&A | 1 hr |
| 17:30 – 17:45 | Wrap-up + next steps | 15 min |

### 2-Day Hackathon (2 × 8 hours)

**Day 1**

| Time | Phase | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 – 09:30 | Welcome + Brief deep-dive | 30 min |
| 09:30 – 12:00 | Ideation (brainstorm → score → decide) | 2.5 hrs |
| 12:00 – 13:00 | 🍕 Lunch | 1 hr |
| 13:00 – 15:30 | Build — Sprint 1 | 2.5 hrs |
| 15:30 – 15:45 | ☕ Break | 15 min |
| 15:45 – 17:15 | Build — Sprint 2 | 1.5 hrs |
| 17:15 – 17:30 | Day 1 check-in | 15 min |

**Day 2**

| Time | Phase | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 – 09:15 | Day 2 kick-off + status | 15 min |
| 09:15 – 12:00 | Build — Sprint 3 | 2.75 hrs |
| 12:00 – 13:00 | 🍕 Lunch | 1 hr |
| 13:00 – 14:30 | Demo prep + rehearsal | 1.5 hrs |
| 14:30 – 14:45 | ☕ Break | 15 min |
| 14:45 – 15:45 | Demos + Q&A | 1 hr |
| 15:45 – 16:15 | Wrap-up + next steps | 30 min |
